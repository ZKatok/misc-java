
PROJECT TITLE: 
"Four has four letters conjecture": Roman numerals & NATO phonetic alphabet Edition

LANGUAGE/ENVIRONMENT:
Java/BlueJ

INSPIRATION FOR PROJECT:
	Based on this conjecture: take any English number and write it as a word (e.g. 3 is "three"). Count how many letters that word has ("three" has 5 letters). Continue turning numbers into words and words into numbers in this fashion ("five" has 4 letters, "four" has 4 letters, "four" has 4 letters...). The conjecture is that no matter what number you start with, it will 'collapse' to an infinite loop of four.
	I was curious what would happen if we took Roman numerals (I, II, III, IV, V, etc.), turned each number into its readout as NATO phonetic alphabet representation (e.g. IV turns into "India-Victor"), counted the number of letters in that representation ("India-Victor" has 11 letters), turned that number into Roman numerals...

DISCOVERIES:
	I found that there are basically 3 options for what happens: it 'collapses' into a repeating loop of nine (IX, India-Xray, 9 letters), a repeating loop of twenty-three (XXIII, Xray-Xray-India-India-India, 23 letters), or a loop that alternates between 13 and 19 (XIII, Xray-India-India-India, 19 letters, XIX, Xray-India-Xray, 13 letters). There is sort of a fourth option in which the 13-19 loop starts with 19 instead of with 13.

PURPOSE OF CODE:
	I used the code to do the steps (turn number to Roman numerals to words to number...until a loop is hit) for the numbers 1 through 999. I also used it to keep track of some stats: how many of each type of loop (that is: 9, 13-19, 19-13, or 23), the lowest and highest numbers of steps to run into the loop, and at what inputs the lowest and highest numbers of steps occur.
	Below I have copied what prints into the terminal/console. The stats are all the way at the bottom.
